Huawei Ban is Hurting US Companies
Written by: keshprad

On May 16, 2019, President Trump’s Administration placed restraints on the business American companies are allowed to do with Huawei, a technology company originating from China. The ban prevents American companies from using telecommunications equipment from “blacklisted” companies, like Huawei.

This ban is affecting major companies drastically. One huge example is Broadcom, a chip-making technology company. Broadcom’s CEO, Hock Tan, believes this ban and the overall trade war between US and China is part of the reason their stocks fell 8% in early trading. Tan gives a statement: “With respect to semiconductors, it is clear that the US-China trade conflict including the Huawei export ban is creating economic and political uncertainty and reducing visibility for global customers.”

However, Broadcom isn’t the only company that was majorly effected by the ban; there were many others, but most importantly, in the next few years Huawei is projected to miss their predicted sales by $30 billion. Although Huawei reportedly denies their products being a risk of national security, the government still fears that Beijing could use the equipment to spy on other nations.
